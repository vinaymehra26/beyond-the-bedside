If you're aspiring to get a staff nurse job at any AIIMS institute, you probably already know that clearing NORCET takes more preparation than your semester exams ever did. You may have also started to look for a course by searching on — YouTube, Telegram, a Google search or maybe just asking a senior who's already been through it.

I also did these too and got overwhelmed quickly after knowing there are so many options to choose from. NNL ONE, Concept RNA, NPrep, PW, Adda 247, Utkarsh, Unacademy — every single one claiming to be "the best," every single one multiple packages inside it. I spent more time comparing platforms than I expected to, and I still wasn't sure if I was comparing them in a right way.

This post will save you a lot of time and possibly your money by giving a framework helpful for choosing a suitable online course for yourself.

## First! Know what You Need in the course

Before opening a single course page, it helps to be honest about your own situation, because it changes what "good" even means for you.

**Final-year students** usually need a course that covers the full syllabus, and explains important topics in brief as you likely haven't revised everything yet, so breadth matters more than speed.

**Working nurses** essentially needs recorded lectures and downloadable notes. Duty schedules shift constantly, and a course built around fixed live-class timings just won't work for you.

**Re-attempters** usually get most benefit from having a strong test series and quick, high-yield revision of the major topics. Detailed test analysis might also be helpful in figuring out your weak spots.

## What you should compare before paying

Here is what is you mainly need to watch for while comparing course.

- **Live vs Recorded vs Hybrid:** If you're working, recorded access isn't optional — it's your major need.
- **Question Banks:** Big question banks are surely useful and attractive but whether the course really provide the updated questions for the latest exam pattern like image-based and clinical-scenario questions is what makes it effective.
- **The test series structure:** How many full-length prelims and mains mocks, whether there are subject-wise sectional tests, and whether you get any performance analytics afterward (useful for re-attempters).
- **PYQs:** Watch for whether the courses include the PYQs or not, and whether the answers are explained or just listed.
- **Language of instruction.** A lot of these courses run in Hinglish by default — worth knowing before you buy if you'd rather study in Hindi only or English only.
- **Access window.** Almost all of the courses access is time limited and can vary from months to a couple of years. So comparing the validity of the course and being sure that it will stick till the exam is crucial.

## What I'd tell a junior asking me directly

Don't start by asking "which course is best." Start by deciding how and when you're are gonna start your preparation for NORCET. And choose the course that's fits with your specific needs. And finally, actual consistent study and mock test are more important than wherever from you're studying.
